1. Introduction

This Order Execution Policy ("Policy") explains the steps fx777 takes to obtain the best possible result for clients when executing their orders. This Policy applies to all retail and professional clients and covers all financial instruments offered by fx777.

fx777 operates a No Dealing Desk (NDD) model, meaning your orders are passed directly to liquidity providers without manual intervention, ensuring fast, fair and transparent execution.

2. Best Execution Obligation

fx777 is required under applicable regulations (including MiFID II and FCA rules) to take all sufficient steps to obtain the best possible result for clients when executing orders. We consider the following factors:

  • Price: The best available bid/offer price from our liquidity pool
  • Costs: Total transaction costs including spreads and commissions
  • Speed: The time taken from order submission to execution
  • Likelihood of execution: The probability that the order will be executed at the requested price
  • Likelihood of settlement: The probability that the trade will settle correctly
  • Size and nature: The order size and the type of instrument being traded

For retail clients, price and cost are typically the most important factors in achieving best execution.

3. Execution Venues

fx777 acts as principal counterparty to all client trades. We source prices from a pool of major global liquidity providers including tier-1 banks and non-bank market makers. Our technology aggregates the best available prices to provide competitive bid/offer quotes at all times.

Our primary liquidity providers include major institutions from the interbank FX market, ensuring deep liquidity and tight spreads across all instruments.

4. Execution Model

4.1 No Dealing Desk (NDD)

fx777 uses a No Dealing Desk execution model for most instruments. Orders are processed automatically through our technology without manual dealer intervention, ensuring speed and eliminating the potential for dealer bias.

4.2 Instant Execution

For MetaTrader accounts, instant execution is available where orders are filled at the requested price or a requote is issued if the price has moved. Clients can accept or reject the requote.

4.3 Market Execution

For cTrader and some MT5 accounts, market execution is used where orders are filled at the best available price at the time of execution. This eliminates requotes but may result in slight slippage during volatile market conditions.

5. Slippage

Slippage occurs when an order is executed at a different price than requested, typically during periods of high market volatility or low liquidity. fx777's execution technology is designed to minimise slippage and provide symmetrical slippage (both positive and negative) to ensure fairness.

6. Order Types

  • Market Order: Executed immediately at the current market price
  • Limit Order: Executed only at the specified price or better
  • Stop Order: Triggered when the market reaches a specified price
  • Stop Loss: Automatically closes a position at a specified loss level
  • Take Profit: Automatically closes a position at a specified profit level
  • Trailing Stop: A dynamic stop loss that follows the market price

7. Conflicts of Interest

As fx777 acts as counterparty to client trades, there is an inherent conflict of interest. We manage this through our NDD model, competitive pricing, transparent execution statistics, and our regulatory obligations to act in clients' best interests at all times.

8. Monitoring and Review

fx777 regularly monitors execution quality and reviews this Policy at least annually. We publish execution quality statistics in accordance with regulatory requirements to provide full transparency to our clients.
