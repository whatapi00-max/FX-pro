Energy Trading FAQs

What drives oil prices?

Oil prices are driven by OPEC production decisions, global economic growth, geopolitical events in oil-producing regions, US dollar strength, and changes in supply and demand fundamentals.

What is the difference between WTI and Brent Crude?

WTI (West Texas Intermediate) is the US benchmark crude, while Brent is the global benchmark produced in the North Sea. Both are widely traded, with Brent typically priced slightly higher.
