EUR/USD Breaks Key Resistance as Euro Strengthens

JD
John Davis • Senior Analyst
• July 11, 2026 • 5 min read

The Euro has gained significant momentum against the US Dollar, breaking through the critical 1.0900 resistance level. This breakout suggests further upside potential as technical indicators align with fundamental factors supporting the single currency.

Technical Analysis

EUR/USD has successfully breached the 1.0900 psychological resistance level, which had capped gains for several sessions. The pair is now targeting the next resistance at 1.0950, with the 1.1000 level serving as a major psychological barrier.

Key Technical Indicators:

  • RSI (14): Currently at 62, indicating bullish momentum without being overbought
  • MACD: Bullish crossover with increasing histogram, confirming upward momentum
  • Moving Averages: Price trading above both 50-day and 200-day MA, confirming the uptrend
  • Support Levels: 1.0850 (immediate), 1.0800 (major)
  • Resistance Levels: 1.0950 (near-term), 1.1000 (psychological)

Fundamental Drivers

The Euro's strength can be attributed to several fundamental factors:

1. ECB Policy Expectations

Market participants are increasingly pricing in a more hawkish stance from the European Central Bank. Recent comments from ECB officials suggest that interest rates may remain elevated for longer than previously anticipated, supporting the Euro.

2. US Dollar Weakness

The US Dollar has shown signs of weakness as Federal Reserve officials have signaled a more patient approach to future rate cuts. This has reduced the yield advantage that had previously supported the Dollar.

3. Economic Data

Recent Eurozone economic data has been better than expected, particularly in the manufacturing and services sectors. This has improved growth outlook for the region, attracting capital flows into the Euro.

Trading Strategy

Based on the current technical and fundamental outlook, we recommend the following approach:

  • Long Positions: Consider long entries on pullbacks to 1.0850-1.0870 support zone
  • Stop Loss: Place stops below 1.0800 to manage risk
  • Take Profit: Target 1.0950 initially, with potential extension to 1.1000
  • Risk Management: Maintain proper position sizing and risk-reward ratio of at least 1:2

Risk Factors

Traders should be aware of the following risks that could reverse the current bullish trend:

  • Unexpected hawkish commentary from Federal Reserve officials
  • Weaker-than-expected Eurozone economic data
  • Geopolitical tensions affecting risk sentiment
  • Breakdown below 1.0800 support level

Conclusion

EUR/USD has demonstrated strong bullish momentum with the successful breakout above 1.0900. The confluence of supportive technical indicators and favorable fundamental factors suggests further upside potential. However, traders should remain vigilant of the risk factors outlined above and maintain proper risk management practices.
